Guide

Don't worry, there is always a first time

Welcome to the Fresher's Guide, a section of FreshStart dedicated to providing you with all the essential information you need as a new undergraduate. From orientation week to your first exams, we've got you covered. Our comprehensive guide includes tips on how to make friends, manage your finances, stay healthy, and much more.

No matter what, we'll be there to guide you

  • Contact home to let them know you have arrived in the UK

    Make a quick phone call, text or email any friends or family to let them know you have safely arrived in the UK. Buy a local SIM card Once you have arrived and settled in, it will be useful to buy a local SIM card to use in your phone.

  • Accommodation

    As you arrive in UK, one of the first things you'll need to do is find your accommodation and collect the key. Your accommodation confirmation email will have all the information you need on how to do this.
    We will keep this section of the checklist updated with any changes to quarantine measures that might affect you. If you're required to quarantine upon arrival in UK, we will make sure that you have everything you need to do so safely and comfortably.

  • Collect your BRP

    You must collect your BRP within 10 days of arriving in the UK. If you do not, you may be fined £800 and your visa could be cancelled.

  • Complete online enrolment and check your timetable

    For new students, this important step helps you officially become part of the University of Wolverhampton; after you complete online enrolment, you will be able to access your course timetable and other University systems. This process is vital in order to maintain an organised and smooth-running institution, but it can be daunting for new starters who are not used to university bureaucracy. Do not worry – here is a quick guide on how to get through it!
    The first thing you'll need when starting university is your student ID card. Your faculty will let you know where to pick it up. You'll need to have this card on hand whenever you want to borrow books from the library or use other university services.

  • Right to study

    You'll need to complete the right to study check before you can enroll online. This is just a quick process to make sure that you're eligible to study at the university, and it's easy enough to do. Just follow these simple steps and you'll be able to enroll in no time. You should receive a personalized link for the right to study check, which will be emailed to you before you complete online enrolment. If you are a student visa holder, you cannot complete the right to study check until you have arrived in the UK.

  • Register with GP

    In addition to registering with a local GP, international students should also make sure they have health insurance that covers them during their stay in the UK. Some universities offer their own health insurance plans, so it's worth checking if your institution provides this service. If not, there are private health insurance options available that cater specifically to international students. It's important to research and purchase a plan before arriving in the UK to ensure you're covered from day one.

  • Open a Bank Account

    How to open bank account? Choose a bank You should research different banks to decide which one best meets your needs. Consider factors such as: Location of branches and ATMs (cash machines) Fees and charges for services you are likely to use, such as account maintenance fees or overdrafts (if applicable) Online banking facilities. You may be required to visit a branch of the bank you have chosen and take documents that show who you are, such as: • Your passport
    • Your BRP
    • Proof of your UK address (for example, a tenancy agreement)
    • Letter from the University of Wolverhampton confirming your status as a UK student If you need a letter from the University of Wolverhampton confirming your student status, please email Student Records with your request.
    Once you have received the necessary documents, you can visit a bank branch to open your account. It is important to note that some banks may require you to make an appointment in advance. During your appointment, the bank will ask for further information such as your contact details and student status. You may also need to provide proof of income or funding source.

  • Learn about University support and services.

    Here are some of the support services that you can access as a student: Download MYWLV app on your phone for useful University information and tools that are easily accessible wherever you are:
    • access your timetable
    • find your lecture room
    • check into online lectures
    • find your modules & courses
    • Student Check-in • Mail

  • Attend social events and make new friends.

    In addition to social support, we also offer academic support services to help you achieve your full potential. Our academic advisors are available to provide guidance on course selection, study skills and time management. We also have a writing center where you can receive feedback and assistance with your written assignments. For students who need additional support, our counseling services team is here to help. They offer confidential counseling sessions for a range of issues including stress, anxiety and depression. We understand that financial concerns can be a worry for some students. That’s why we have a financial aid office where you can get advice on student loans, scholarships and other funding options. Finally, we take the safety of our students seriously. Our campus security team is available 24/7 to ensure the safety of all members of our community. Remember that these support services are here for you whenever you need them. Don’t hesitate to reach out if you need any assistance during your time at university.
